Mac and "Cheese" 2 Ways


Adding an allergy friendly twist to a childhood favorite!


When I was younger I LOVED mac and cheese, and when I discovered my food intolerances, I was pretty upset that I would have to give it up for good. Luckily, I got creative and found so many ways to make non-dairy mac and “cheese” that even squeeze in some extra veggies without sacrificing the flavor! Try both and let me know what you think on my Contact Me page!



Cheesy Pumpkin Pasta with Greens
Ingredients:

1 serving pasta of your choice (I love Banza!)
½ cup pumpkin puree
2 tbsp cashews (soaked)
Splash of non-dairy milk
1 heaping tbsp nutritional yeast
Dried basil and pepper to taste
Salad greens (goes well with balsamic vinegar)

Directions:

  1. Soak cashews for 4-6 hours or quick soak for 30 minutes in boiling water.
  2. Boil water and cook pasta according to directions.
  3. While pasta is cooking, blend together the pumpkin puree, soaked cashews, milk, and nutritional yeast.
  4. Once fully blended set aside until pasta has finished cooking.
  5. Drain pasta and add back to the pot with the freshly blended sauce. Add dried basil and pepper to taste. Enjoy with greens and a balsamic dressing!

Serves 1.


Butternut Squash Mac and Peas
Ingredients:

1 serving pasta of your choice (I love Banza!)
1 serving frozen green peas
1 cup frozen butternut squash
2 tbsp cashews (soaked)
Splash of non-dairy milk
1 heaping tbsp nutritional yeast
Dried basil and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Soak cashews for 4-6 hours or quick soak for 30 minutes in boiling water.
  2. Boil water and cook pasta according to directions.
  3. In the last 3-4 minutes of cooking, add the frozen peas to the boiling water with the pasta.
  4. In the microwave or on the stove, steam butternut squash until soft and easily pierced with a fork.
  5. Allow the butternut squash to cool for a bit before putting in your blender.
  6. Blend together the butternut squash, soaked cashews, milk, and nutritional yeast.
  7. Once fully blended set aside until pasta has finished cooking.
  8. Drain pasta and peas and add back to the pot with the freshly blended sauce. Add dried basil pepper to taste and enjoy!

Serves 1.



Holiday Lemon Shrimp Pasta


Popping with red and green and full of flavor!


This pasta is flavorful, simple, and filled with holiday colors! The sautéed spinach and tomatoes make this pasta pop with red and green, so it always reminds me of the holidays. This meal takes only as long as it takes to boil water and cook pasta – the perfect weekday balanced meal when you want something quick and easy. Simply cook pasta, sauté the veggies, and add some spice! Basil and lemon give this dish its fresh flavor while the pasta makes it the perfect healthy comfort dish.



Ingredients:

1 box spaghetti (I use Banza Pasta for extra protein)
Frozen shrimp
Olive oil
A few handfuls of spinach
Chopped cherry tomatoes
Handful of fresh basil (plus more for topping if desired)
Juice of ½ lemon
Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Boil water and cook the pasta according to directions.
  2. While the pasta is cooking add the frozen shrimp and a little bit of oil to a pan and heat over medium-high heat.
  3. Once the shrimp has cooked for a couple minutes add in the tomatoes and the fresh basil along with the lemon juice.
  4. Once the pasta is done cooking, add in the pasta and spinach and continue to sauté until the spinach is wilted (should only take a couple minutes).
  5. Add salt and pepper to taste and serve!

Serves 2-4 people.



Oat my Goodness!


4 Ways to Make Oats Fun


Oatmeal has to be my all-time favorite food – the amount of combinations you can make with this food is amazing. And the best part is using toppings galore when it comes to oats. In general, breakfast is my favorite meal of the day – oatmeal, pancakes, waffles, yogurt, granola, muffins, you name it. I’m definitely more of a sweet person and so breakfast always leans more on the sweet than savory side - all about balance! To spread my oatmeal love check out these 4 favorite oatmeal recipe combinations, ready whip up in the morning in the microwave or on the stove! **I prefer stove cooked oats in milk instead of water for a creamier texture, but the microwave is much quicker and less clean-up!
For each add 1 part oats to 2 parts water or milk and cook on the stove or microwave for 1.5-3 minutes. Stir in ingredients before cooking and add toppings after. Feel free to get creative with toppings in any way you like! My favorites are nuts, seeds, dried fruit, chopped up protein bars, nut butter, granola, and maple syrup! Just add whatever makes you feel good in the morning!

1. Maca Vanilla



If you’ve never tried Maca powder it smells and tastes similar to butterscotch. I love this superfood and the taste it adds to my oatmeal, especially with a splash of vanilla! Maca is a superfood said to balance out hormones, and while this is important, I eat it mostly for taste!
Ingredients:
Oats and water/milk
1 tablespoon maca powder
Splash of vanilla
Suggested Toppings: berries, coconut, and cashew butter






2. Ginger Molasses Pear



One of my favorite comfort foods is molasses. I love molasses cookies and it always reminds me of the holidays and family time. This bowl of oats is a delicious version of those molasses cookies and is perfect for a rainy day or to kick start any morning!
Ingredients:
Oats and water/milk
1 tablespoon molasses
1 teaspoon ginger
1 tablespoon cinnamon
Dash of nutmeg
Splash of vanilla
Suggested Toppings: pecans, pear, and coconut



3. Chocolate Nut Butter



Chocolate all day every day! If I could incorporate chocolate into every meal I would. I love starting my day with a warm bowl of oats that tastes like a chocolate bar with nut butter melted in the middle. This is sure to get you out of bed in the morning and give you something great to start your day. Plus cocoa powder is great energy booster! **Peep my puppy eyeing my delicious breakfast:)
Ingredients:
Oats and water/milk
1 heaping tablespoon cocoa powder
1 tablespoon maple syrup
Suggested Toppings: nut butter, blueberries, and cocoa nibs






4. Carrot Cake



Recently I’ve fallen in love with carrot cake. And so when I figured out that oats could taste like carrot cake, I feel in love with those too. This bowl of oats packs in lots of nutrients from the carrots and is very filling and satisfying to get you started with your day. Also a great dinner if you’re in a rush and are just feeling a bowl of oats!
Ingredients:
Oats and milk (this one works best soaked overnight or made on the stove – both versions made with milk)
1 cup grated carrot
1 tablespoon maple syrup
Splash of vanilla
1 tablespoon cinnamon
1 teaspoon ginger
2 dashes of nutmeg
Suggested Toppings: walnuts, raisins, hemp seeds, and more cinnamon
